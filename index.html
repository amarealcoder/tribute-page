Lola Omolola

This is a tribute page dedicated to Lola Omolola: The lady who created a niche for women to end the culture of silence.

Photo of Lola Omolola

Lola Omolola (born August 1, 1976) is a Nigerian former journalist who founded the Female IN (FIN) group on Facebook. Lola is the first Nigerian woman who created a place where other women can share their untold stories regarding their sexual abuses, and other challenges they are facing. She was featured in the 2018 Facebook F8 Conference.

Though alive and young, Omolola has made quite an impact in her immediate community.

Education

Omolola completed her primary and secondary studies in Nigeria. After moving to the United States, she earned a Bachelor's degree in Broadcast journalism from Columbia College in Chicago.

Career

Omolola is a former journalist, and she owned some TV shows in Nigeria. After earning her degree, she worked at the Community Counseling Centers of Chicago where she assisted people with mental health issues. Also, Omolola worked for "apartments.com" but decided to quit when she had children. As a result, she started her own website called "spicebaby.com" where she was providing recipes for Nigerian foods. Then she created FIN which is a Facebook private group page where women all over the world meet and share their stories without embarrassment. The group started as Female in Nigeria, but now changed to "Female IN" to accommodate its increasingly diverse membership. FIN has more than a million women who use this Facebook page. Omolola's goal is to help women who are struggling with life, but can not tell anyone about their issues because of tradition. In many countries like Nigeria, girls and women are voiceless. FIN has given a lot of women the platorm and courage to voice out their frustrations,disappointments, abuses, harrassments as wellas their happiness and breakthroughs in life without the fear of being judged. Thus, helping to end the culture of silence for women.

Achievements

After she created FIN, Omolola met the Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg. They discussed how women are getting support from FIN. In an interview with CNN, Mark Zuckerberg praised her for connecting voiceless women and building a safe community for them on Facebook. Lola is now planning to move forward with FIN by "providing centres where women can go to talk about their experiences in a safe space."
